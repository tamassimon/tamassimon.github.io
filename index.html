Failure Résumé

eBooks

Before ePub became a standard back in 2006 I was very excited about eInk and ebooks. I couldn't wait for the SONY Reader to be sold in North America - as it was already being sold in Japan.

I thought the ebook business would play out similarly to the music business and a couple of big companies would be sharing the "pie". Looking at Amazon+Kindle and Apple+iBooks I wasn't far off.
I thought there would be space for an independent entity - much like the idea of eMusic in the music world who only go after 1-2% market share and offer DRM free music.
I started working on some ideas and built DigitalReading.net (no longer online ) using Drupal and various add-on modules. The google Custom Search I built to allow people to find ebooks got Google's attention: http://librariancentral.blogspot.ca/2007/02/create-google-custom-search-engine.html (also no longer online)

Proof: I was an active contributor to the TeleRead blog: http://www.teleread.com/author/tamas/
